Jiangnan 江南

Jiangnan Sizhu（江南丝竹） is a traditional form of folk music from the Jiangsu and Zhejiang regions of China, characterized by its rich cultural heritage and unique regional features. This music typically employs silk and bamboo instruments（丝竹乐器）, including the erhu, guzheng, dizi, and suona, creating a gentle and beautiful playing style often accompanied by flowing melodies and rhythms.

The name "Sizhu" literally means "silk and bamboo," referring to the materials traditionally used to make the instruments. Silk strings are used for instruments like the erhu（二胡） and pipa（琵琶）, while bamboo is used for wind instruments like the dizi（笛子） and xiao（箫）. This ensemble music is often performed in teahouses, gardens, and during traditional festivals.
